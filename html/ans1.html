The fractions we're adding: they are getting smaller. The tops are 1. The bottoms are doubled each time or they are powers of 2. 8= 2^3, 16 = 2^4, and so on.

The partial sums: They are getting bigger. If you add the top and bottom, you get the top number in the next partial sum. The top number is always 1 less than the bottom number. The bottom number is always a power of 2. We can write the nth partial sum as (2^n - 1)/2^n. Some people say that there is always one of the fraction left over, so the nth partial sum can be written as 1 - 1/(2^n). I say the infinite series goes to 1. (As n-> infinity, 1/(2^n) ->0, so 1-1/(2^n)->1 and (2^n-1)/2^n -> 1 and mathematicians say the limit of these is 1; and the limit of this series is 1).
(With young people I don't introduce the word limit right away).
