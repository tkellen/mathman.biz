Vignettes- short interesting works

1. Mark is a physicist (among other things) and the father of Kaitlin, one of Don's students. Kaitlin, a 4th grader, made up a quadratic equation for Don, after doing five or six herself. Then when her Dad came to pick her up, she gave it to her Dad to do. The equation she made up was

x2  -12x + 32 = 0

Now Caitlin knew the answers were 8 and 4, because she figured out the secrets, that  8x4 = 32 and 8+4 =12.

Mark did this: he subtracted 32 from each side, then factored the x out on the left. So he had

x2  -12x = - 32   and 

x(x-12) =  - 32

 He then figured that 8 would work because 8*(8 -12) = 8* - 4 = - 32

and 4 works also because 4* - 8 = - 32.

Another way to solve a quadratic equation! Don has about 10 ways to solve a quadratic equation in his books (See Ch. 8).
