MarieK shares 8 cookies between 3 people, and

Lori Johnson Morse helps us get a video of it.

Marie had been cookie-sharing for weeks, getting infinite series for some. The evening before this video her Dad had shown her how to divide 8 by 3 and talked about other things as shown below. This was the page Don and Marie were referring to during the video.

Lori Johnson Morse had come from KC, MO, with her husband Joe, to visit Don, observe and talk about math stuff  She took on the job of  videotaping Don and Marie as Marie shared 8 cookies between 3 people. The exciting thing that happened was that, a first for Don, Marie, in sharing the cookies, divided each leftover cookie into 10 equal pieces!   No one in Don's 58 years of teaching math had ever done this. Why is this so exciting? Because one gets the infinite series 2 + 6/10 + 6/100 + 6/1000 +...as well as the infinite repeating decimal = 2.666..., as well as the mixed number =2 2/3

Now the video is 11+ minutes. And there was a lot going on: Don wanted to capture a 7 year old doing all of this (cutting leftover cookies  into 10 pieces(!)).

So the important things to remember are:

1. The problem was to share 8 cookies between 3 people.

2. Everyone got 2 cookies, and there were 2 cookies left over.

3. Marie cut the 2 leftover cookies into 10 pieces (tenths).

4. There were 20 tenths to share

5. Each person got 6 tenths of a cookie

6. And there were 2 tenths left over

7. Then Marie cut each tenth into 10 pieces, to get 20 hundredths.

This is where the video begins! Where Don tries to start again for the camera, and then again when Sydney's Mom comes in to pick her up, and again when Marie's Dad comes in to pick Marie and her sister Ann up. And Marie's Dad wanted to know what happens when the girls stop coming to see Don. (They later said they would be returning in November). And Lori did a fantastic job of videotaping for our amateur movie.

Then this repeats to get 6 thousandths... and to get the infinite series 2 + 6/10 + 6/100 + ...=  2.666... an infinite repeating decimal  = 2 2/3, a mixed number and the fraction 8/3!!
