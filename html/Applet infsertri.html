14 August 2008 Look at and try the applet that Don and Lori created while she was here and finished at this date, using  GeoGebra. This applet shows Don's discovery that the sum, or limit, of an infinite series equals the area of a triangle.  You can choose the numerator(a) and the denominator(b) of the fraction A/B. Notice that the base of the triangle is twice the infinite sum.

  The limit of an infinite series = the Area of a triangle
